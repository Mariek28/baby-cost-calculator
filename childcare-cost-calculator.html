Understanding childcare costs

Childcare is often the largest baby-related expense after housing, and the price can vary dramatically by location, schedule, and type of care. A daycare center may charge a weekly or monthly tuition, while a nanny usually charges hourly wages. Home daycare may be less expensive than a center, but availability and licensing rules vary. A nanny share can reduce the cost of nanny care by splitting the caregiver's time with another family, while family care may be free or reduced but still deserves clear communication about schedules, expectations, and backup plans.

Hours per week matter. Part-time care can still be expensive if the provider has a minimum tuition requirement. Full-time care at forty to fifty hours per week can affect not only the weekly fee but also overtime, late pickup charges, meals, supplies, and transportation. More than one child in care may increase costs, although some providers offer sibling discounts. This calculator uses average cost assumptions so you can compare options and then replace the numbers with quotes from local providers.

The after-tax estimate is a reminder to ask about dependent care benefits. A dependent care FSA may reduce taxable income for eligible families, but rules and limits change and depend on your situation. Treat this section as a planning prompt, not tax advice. Always confirm details with your employer, benefits administrator, or tax professional before making decisions.

Childcare Cost FAQ

What type of childcare is cheapest?

Family care and home daycare are often less expensive, but cost depends heavily on location and availability.

Is a nanny more expensive than daycare?

A full-time nanny is usually more expensive because one caregiver is dedicated to one family.

What is a nanny share?

A nanny share is when two families share one nanny and split some of the cost.

Can childcare costs vary by state?

Yes. State, city, licensing rules, wages, and demand all affect prices.

What is a dependent care FSA?

It is a workplace benefit that may let eligible families set aside pre-tax money for qualifying care expenses.
